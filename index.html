Seasonal & Sub-Seasonal Tropical Cyclone Forecasts

Forecasting

Colorado State University has issued forecasts of Atlantic basin hurricane activity since 1984. Following the pioneering work of Prof. William Gray, our group continues the long CSU tradition in forecasting with continually improving techniques for predicting tropical cyclone activity powered by new research.

Learn More

Team Lead: Jennifer DeHart

Software and Artificial Intelligence

The Software and Artificial Intelligence subgroup develops new tools that improve data processing workflows and enable cutting-edge research. These tools include open source software for processing and analyzing weather lidar and radar data, an automated radar quality control algorithm to bypass time-consuming manual data editing, and an algorithm to estimate surface wind speeds from flight level observations in tropical cyclones.

Learn More

Team Lead: Brenda Dolan

Radar and Field Experiments

The use of weather radar data is ingrained in the Bell group research in a variety of facets. We use radar data to study microphysics and dynamics associated with convection, cloud organization, and precipitation. We also collect data from around the world through participating in or leading field campaigns to better understand these processes in the atmosphere.

Learn More

Team Lead: Levi Silvers

Atmospheric Modeling

Our research team uses a combinatino of theory and numerical models to explore t he dominant physical processes and the interactions between hurricanes, tropical waves and the variability of the climate system. The primary targets of our research include developing an improved understanding of the early stages of tropical cyclone development, rapid intensifiaction of h urricanes, the the role that ENSO plays in the variability of tropical cyclones.
